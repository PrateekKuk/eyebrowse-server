About Eyebrowse

Eyebrowse aims to create a social outdoors for your web browsing.

Managing your sharelist

A sharelist is a list of domains that you are interested in being publicly seen on and you are comfortable sharing your activity on. You control what domains are on your sharelist. Some typical domains that users sharelist include Wikipedia, CNN, or StackOverflow, just as examples.

Using the browser plugin that we've created for Chrome, it will occasionally ask you while you are browsing the Internet whether you would like to share the domain that you are currently on. You can also add a domain to your sharelist without being prompted by clicking on the Eyebrowse Chrome extension and selecting 'Share this Domain'.

You can always add more domains or remove domains from your sharelist by editing your profile on the Eyebrowse website. Once you've added a domain to your sharelist that that you are comfortable sharing your activity on, the plugin will track when you visit a page on that domain and post it to your Eyebrowse feed for you automatically.

If you want to mark a visit to a webpage just this one time without putting the domain in your sharelist, you can do this from within the Eyebrowse plugin by clicking "Mark a visit to this page" while on that page. Finally, if you ever want to not be tracked on any sites for a short while, you can click "Eyebrowse: On" on the Eyebrowse plugin to toggle it on and off. Any browsing you do in Chrome incognito mode will by default have Eyebrowse off.


Social activities in the plugin

Anytime you are browsing the web, you can "bump into" other people or cross paths with others. Eyebrowse notifies you of this by showing in the extension popup all the other people currently on the same page and domain, as well as people who have been to this page before.

You can post Notes, which are short messages or comments of your choosing, to any page on the web. You can think of Notes as being similar to a news article comment, Facebook status post, or tweet. To post a Note, click on the Eyebrowse logo in your Chrome browser. Bulletins will also post to your Eyebrowse feed on the website and from there, to the home feeds of your followers. You can also view all Notes on a page's Bulletin Board on the Chrome Extension popup or on the Eyebrowse website.

Additionally, you can post Chats, more light-weight discussions with people within a page. These discussions, while public, do not get posted to your feed, and people can only see them when they are on that page. To see and participate in Chats, open the Chrome extension and find the Chat Room within the popup.

While you are browsing the web, small popups will appear under the eyebrowse extension logo. If someone else has been on the same page or domain in the last month, their icon will show up in the popup. The more transparent their logo is, the longer it's been since they've been here. If there is a yellow border around their icon, that means you have bumped into this person - you two are on the same page at the same time. Also, the small popup will show a snippet of the most recent Note or Chat message posted. If you're interested, you can click the extension icon to read more. If you want to get rid of the popup, you can click on the message and it will disappear, or wait 4 seconds for it to fade away.


Social activities on the website

On the Eyebrowse website, your homepage is a feed of all the people you are following. You can see the webpages that they've visited on their sharelist, and you can click through to view those webpages and discover interesting content. You can also view the Firehose of all Eyebrowse visits and search within it to find content or new people to follow. There are also aggregate statistics on the top links and most visited domains in the last day and week. You can browse other people's profile pages and view people's followers and followees. You can edit your own profile and manage your sharelist.

If you click on Notifications in the left part of the navigation bar, you can see interesting things that have happened on the pages you visited in the last week. This page will tell you if you've bumped into anyone while browsing as well as any Notes or Chat messages that have been posted in the last week on pages you've visited.



About Us

We are a team of researchers and students at MIT CSAIL and members of the Haystack Research Group at MIT. Our overall research goal is to make it easier for people to collect, organize, find, visualize, and share information. Eyebrowse is pursuing this goal for web activity and traversal. Principle members of this team currently include:
