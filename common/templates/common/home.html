Welcome to Eyebrowse!


When we go outside, a lot of the fun comes from seeing what other people are doing. We can follow (or steer away from) the crowd. We can bump into friends. We can simply sit back and enjoy observing the ebb and flow of people.

Wouldn't it be interesting to do the same on the web?

Eyebrowse aims to create a social outdoors for your web browsing.

Share.

By making yourself visible on sites you designate, you open up a wealth of new social opportunities. You can chat in real-time or post comments to any webpage you wish, not just the ones that have those features enabled. You'll be able to "bump into people" and notice when your friends are on the same sites as you. Your friend's browsing paths can lead you to interesting new web content, and yours can lead them. You can see places that are attracting a crowd while it's happening, or follow well-trodden paths.

Take control.

As an Eyebrowse user, you specify the websites you wish to be visible on, and those are the only ones where you are visible to other users and they can see where you've been. We will never track your visits to websites that you have not explicitly given permission to be visible on.

Contribute.

As a community, we'll be able to build up a publicly visible map of the ebbs and flows of traffic on the web. By contributing your data to this public repository of web activity, you'll make new research and cool projects possible. We're excited about all the great things that can be learned or apps or visualizations that can be built from this data, which is why we have an API for you to use.

Curious? Read more at the About page or our FAQ.
