What is Eyebrowse?

Eyebrowse is a social web browsing tool. It allows you to pick websites that you consider like public spaces in real life and makes you publicly visible on them. Just like in real life, you can bump into friends, chat with others, and see what others are doing while browsing the web. The tool is made up of a website where you have a profile and you can view other people's eyebrowse history, as well as a Chrome browser plugin where you can see, while you're browsing the parts of the web you consider public, the other people that are on the same webpage as you and chat or leave comments on any pages.

 

Why would I use Eyebrowse?

There are a lot of fun and interesting uses and opportunities that Eyebrowse can provide. First, you can now have discussions and leave notes on any webpage, not just the ones that have those features enabled. You can get a picture of your own browsing habits as well as how other people in the world are browsing the web, all in real time. For instance, you could discover where crowds are congregating on the web or find some well-trodden paths. There are so many possibilities, which is why we have released an API for you to use if you are interested. You can download your own data or the entire Firehose and uncover interesting findings or build cool visualizations. You can even build a new app that connects to the Eyebrowse API. An additional benefit to contributing your own data is that you help researchers working on important problems related to information retrieval, social computing, and human-computer interaction.

 

What's your motivation for building Eyebrowse?

We have built Eyebrowse for many reasons.

First, we're really interested in the possibilities for social interaction, sharing, and content discovery that Eyebrowse can provide. We also think that there is a lot of opportunity in being able to visualize the paths and ebbs and flows of traffic on the web.

Second, in building this system, we wish to demonstrate that it is possible to have a system that asks users permission before tracking them instead of indiscriminately tracking all websites that users go to, including the ones that they prefer to keep private. We believe that there are some websites that users do not mind being tracked on and others where they do mind, but it is currently cumbersome or difficult for users to take control. This system is a proof-of-concept to show that browser tracking can be permission-based without being annoying or tiresome for users.

Finally, we hope by releasing the data that Eyebrowse collects, we can help researchers, data scientists, software engineers, and designers learn and create interesting and useful things. We think that areas like quantified self, information retrieval, social computing, human-computer interaction, and many more could benefit from the data we collect.

 

How much of my browsing data does Eyebrowse collect?

Eyebrowse only collects data from the domains that you give it permission to collect. You give Eyebrowse permission when you add a domain to your sharelist. After you've done that, anytime you visit a webpage that is a part of that domain, you will show up as present on that domain within the Eyebrowse Chrome plugin, and your Eyebrowse feed will register a visit to that webpage.

 

Is the data I put on my Eyebrowse feed public?

Yes. At this moment, all the data that goes to your Eyebrowse is publicly available, as in any person can create an Eyebrowse account and see it. Likewise, your data will show up in the public API and in the link to the Eyebrowse firehose from your home page. Only add domains to your sharelist that you are comfortable sharing publicly.

 

Exactly what fields are collected with each visit?

From every visit that is collected from Eyebrowse, we collect the time you entered the page and the time you leave the page. From the webpage, we store the url, the domain, the title, and the favIcon of the domain. Finally, the visit is associated with your Eyebrowse account. We don't store any other information such as IP address or referrer. We also don't track the clicks that you make while on a shared domain.

 

Is it possible for me to use Eyebrowse anonymously?

It is possible for you to create an account and not use a username or profile image that links back to you or reveal your email. However, it is often easy to reveal your identity from your online traces without realizing it. Therefore, we can not guarantee that your anonymity will be preserved if you are sharing any domains. We recommend that you only share the domains that you are comfortable associating with yourself.

 

Can I delete my data on Eyebrowse?

If you ever want to delete your entire account or remove certain Eyebrowse history items from your Eyebrowse feed, you can do so from the Eyebrowse website. Just click on the down arrow to the right of the visit on your feed and one of the options will be deletion. Once you do that, there is no undo-ing. The data will be permanently removed from Eyebrowse and will not be on the Eyebrowse website or appear in the API. However, we cannot guarantee that your data will be entirely gone as any people collecting information in real-time from our API could have potentially already downloaded that data before we went ahead and deleted it.

 

How do I add a website to my sharelist?

There are several ways you can add a website to your sharelist. The first way is by simply browsing the web with the Eyebrowse Chrome extension on. Occasionally, the plugin will ask you via a small window in the top right of your browser window whether you would like to add the current domain you are on to your sharelist If you click yes, that current visit and future visits to webpages within that domain will be logged to Eyebrowse. If you click no, the extension will not ask you about that domain again.

If at any time you want to share the domain you are currently on but you didn't receive a prompt, you can click on the Eyebrowse extension logo and then click "Share this domain".

Finally, you can go to Settings in the top right corner of the Eyebrowse website and then click on Sharelist. From here, you can add and delete items that are on your sharelist.

 

How do I delete a website from my sharelist?

If you go to Settings in the top right corner of the Eyebrowse website and then click on Sharelist, you can see all the websites that are currently in your sharelist. Click the red X button in order to remove an item from your sharelist.

 

Can I mark a visit to a webpage without putting it on my sharelist

Yes, you can. If you are currently on a webpage that's not on your sharelist, but you want to mark your current visit just this one time without putting the webpage onto your sharelist, simply click "Mark a visit to this page" from within the Eyebrowse Chrome extension.

 

How do I temporarily turn off Eyebrowse?

If, while you're browsing the web, decide that you want to go incognito and stop Eyebrowse from logging any of your shared domains or showing you as online on Eyebrowse or any webpage, you can go incognito. Simply click the "Eyebrowse: On" link from the Chrome extension to toggle Eyebrowse on or off. When incognito mode is on, the eye in the Chrome extension icon should look closed. You can still mark a visit to a particular site, publish a Note, or post a Chat message while you're incognito. Don't forget to toggle Eyebrowse back on when you're ready to join the public!

 

What's the difference between a Note and a Chat message and how do I post one?

You can post either a Note or a Chat message anytime you are browsing the web. Simply click on the Eyebrowse Chrome extension icon, and there are input text fields for both. You can also see the previous Notes on the Bulletin Board and Chat messages in the Chat Room that were posted to that page before you. Both Notes and Chat messages are publicly accessible to all Eyebrowse users. When you publish a Note to a webpage, it will appear both in your feed on the Eyebrowse website as well as in the browser extension when you are on that webpage. When you post a Chat message, it only posts to the webpage. So when you are have a conversation, for instance in real-time with other people on a webpage, it would make more sense to post Chat messages. When you want to broadcast a statement to your followers about a webpage like you would a Twitter or Facebook post, it would make more sense to publish a Note.

 

Is it possible to delete or edit Notes or Chat messages?

Yes! If there are some Eyebrowse visits or Notes you would really like to be deleted, there is a down-arrow to the right of items in your feed on the website. Click and from there you can delete anything from a particular link or entire domain. However, as explained here, we cannot guarantee that your data is completely gone. Currently there is no way to delete Chat messages.
