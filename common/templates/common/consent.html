Before you continue...

Thank you for using Eyebrowse!
Eyebrowse is a research project from MIT CSAIL's Haystack Group exploring the possibilities of social web browsing.

Part of what we are seeking to learn are:
- How users of Eyebrowse use the system on a day-to-day basis, and
- Whether features in Eyebrowse are useful or interesting to participants.

As you should know by now, much information on Eyebrowse is publicly available. This includes the browser visits to webpages on websites you have whitelisted, comments you post, and chats you post.

We are additionally collecting some information on usage of the Eyebrowse system such as the following:
- How many times do people click on a link on the Eyebrowse stream?
- What domains do people add to their whitelist?
- What domains do people expressly not want to be tracked on?
- What pages on the Eyebrowse website are most visited?

We will not be making this information publicly available on the website. We wish to analyze the data to help answer our questions above and make Eyebrowse a better tool. If we do present this data, we will only do it in an aggregate manner and fully anonymized.

Guarantees to you as a participant in this research project:
- Your participation is voluntary. You may uninstall Eyebrowse and abandon the application at any time.
- Please email eyebrowse@mit.edu with any concerns or questions.
